Eschirichia coli

E.coli bacterias are normal G.I.T flora. They cause infection in both humans and animals. They are gram negative, non-spore forming, facultative anaerobes. They are rod shaped and motile. They ferment lactose to produce gas. They are oxidase negative and catalase positive. They are also urease negative.

They are transmitted through the fecal-oral route. They are also transmitted through contaminated food and water. They cause gastroenteritis, urinary tract infections, and meningitis in newborns. They are treated with antibiotics such as ampicillin, ciprofloxacin, and trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole.

They are classified into various pathotypes as follows;

  1. Enterotoxigenic E.coli (ETEC)
  2. Enteropathogenic E.coli (EPEC)
  3. Enteroaggregative E.coli (EAEC)
  4. Enteroinvasive E.coli (EIEC)
  5. Shiga toxin-producing E.coli (STEC)
